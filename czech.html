Were Pfizer and Moderna vaccines allocated randomly?

Kirsch wrote: "Vaccines were randomly distributed for those wishing to get vaxxed. [...] People were not allowed to select which vaccines they got. [...] The randomization of which vaccine someone got created a perfect real-world randomized clinical trial where we could compute the mortality rates for 1 year after Dose 2 for the two most popular vaccines." [https://github.com/skirsch/Czech]

However I didn't find any source which said that the vaccines were actually allocated randomly in the Czech Republic.

In the FOIA data the average year of birth is about 1973 for people whose vaccine type for the second dose was "Comirnaty" (Pfizer) but about 1966 for "SPIKEVAX" (Moderna), which indicates that the vaccine types were not allocated randomly:

Bucket analysis

The following code generates a file for deaths and person-days by ongoing month and dose number. It's similar to the buckets.py script provided by Kirsch, but it accounts for the aging of people over time correctly. In buckets.py the age of each person is always either the age on the day of death or the age on the day when the script was ran for people who didn't die. [https://github.com/skirsch/Czech/blob/main/code/buckets.py]

The FOIA data only has a year of birth for each person but not a date of birth, so here I generated a random date of birth for each person.

library(data.table)

# unique apply (faster for long vectors with many repeated values)
ua=\(x,fun,...){u=unique(x);fun(u,...)[match(x,u)]}

# fast way to get floored number of years between dates after 1900 and before 2100
age=\(x,y){x=as.numeric(x);y=as.numeric(y);(y-x-(y-789)%/%1461+(x-789)%/%1461)%/%365}

mindate=as.Date("2020-1-1");maxdate=as.Date("2022-12-31")

t0=fread("Czech/data/CR_records.csv")
t=data.table(id=1:nrow(t0),death=t0$DatumUmrti)
set.seed(0)
t$birth=ua(ua(t0$Rok_narozeni,paste0,"-1-1"),as.Date)+sample(0:364,nrow(t),T)
t=t[rep(1:nrow(t),8),]
t$dose=rep(0:7,each=nrow(t0))
t$date=c(rep(mindate,nrow(t0)),ua(unlist(lapply(1:7,\(i)t0[[paste0("Datum_",i)]])),as.Date,"1970-1-1"))
k=paste0("OckovaciLatka_",1:7);t$type=c(rep("",nrow(t0)),unlist(t0[,..k]))

t=t[!is.na(date)][date<=maxdate][order(-date)]

buck=data.table()
for(day in as.list(seq(mindate,maxdate,1))){
  cat(as.character(day),"\n")
  sub=t[date<=day&(is.na(death)|death>=day)&birth<=day]
  sub=unique(sub,by="id") # remove this line to keep people included under previous doses after a new dose
  d=data.table(date=day,dose=sub$dose,age=age(sub$birth,day),alive=1)
  d$dead=nafill(as.numeric(sub$death==day),,0)
  buck=rbind(buck,d)[,.(alive=sum(alive),dead=sum(dead)),by=.(date,dose,age)]
}

fwrite(buck,"czbuckets",sep=" ")

I uploaded the output here: f/czbuckets.gz (about 2 MB).

Plot for ASMR by dose and date

In the plot below the ASMR of people with one dose shoots up when the second dose is rolled out, and the ASMR of people with two doses shoots up when the third dose is rolled out. Martin Neil and Norman Fenton speculated that a similar phenomenon in the ONS dataset was explained by the "cheap trick" where people were categorized under the previous vaccine dose for a certain number of weeks after a new vaccine dose, so that for example people who died soon after the second dose were classified as deaths under the first dose. Jeffrey Morris and the ONS said that the phenomenon was explained by the healthy vaccinee effect instead. However a similar phenomenon can also be seen in the Czech data and in the New Zealand data released by Barry Young, even though the "cheap trick" is not used in either dataset, which makes it seem even more likely that Morris was right and Neil and Fenton were wrong.

During a COVID wave in December 2021, unvaccinated people have a big spike in mortality but there is essentially no spike in mortality in people with 3 doses. People with 2 doses do have a big increase in mortality in December 2021, but it's probably because a lot of people got the third dose in December 2021 so the "unhealthy stragglers" ended up remaining under the second dose.

In the plot above the dark gray line shows the mortality rate among both vaccinated and unvaccinated people based on the FOIA data, and the light gray line shows the mortality rate based on the weekly number of deaths reported by the Czech Statistical Office combined with population estimates from Eurostat. I don't know why the dark gray line is much lower than the light gray line.

library(data.table);library(ggplot2);library(stringr)

cutl=\(x,y)cut(x,c(y,Inf),y,T,F)
isoweek=\(year,week,weekday=1){d=as.Date(paste0(year,"-1-4"));d-(as.integer(format(d,"%w"))+6)%%7-1+7*(week-1)+weekday}
ma=\(x,b=1,f=b)rowMeans(embed(c(rep(NA,b),x,rep(NA,f)),f+b+1),na.rm=T)

dead=read.csv("http://sars2.net/f/czweeklydead.csv")
xstart=as.Date("2020-1-1");xend=as.Date("2022-12-31")
dates=seq(xstart,xend,1)
d=data.frame(date=dates,age=rep(unique(dead$age),each=length(dates)))
d$dead=c(sapply(split(dead,dead$age),\(x)predict(smooth.spline(isoweek(x$year,x$week,4),x$dead),as.numeric(dates))$y))/7

pop=read.csv("http://sars2.net/f/czpopdead.csv")
pop=aggregate(pop[,3,drop=F],list(year=pop$year,age=cutl(pop$age,unique(d$age))),sum)
std=pop$pop[pop$year==2022]
d$pop=c(sapply(split(pop,pop$age),\(x){x$date=as.Date(paste0(x$year,"-1-1"));predict(lm(pop~date,x),list(date=dates))}))

buck=fread("http://sars2.net/f/czbuckets.gz")
buck=buck[,.(alive=sum(alive),dead=sum(dead)),by=.(date,age=cutl(age,unique(d$age)),dose=ifelse(dose==0,"Unvaccinated",paste0("Dose ",ifelse(dose>=4,"4+",dose))))]
buck=rbind(buck,cbind(expand.grid(date=as.IDate(seq(xstart,xend,1)),age=unique(buck$age),dose=unique(buck$dose)),alive=0,dead=0))
buck=unique(buck,by=1:3)[order(dose,age,date)]

vax=cbind(buck[dose!="Unvaccinated",.(alive=sum(alive),dead=sum(dead)),by=.(age=age,date=date)],dose="All vaccinated")
all=cbind(buck[,.(alive=sum(alive),dead=sum(dead)),by=.(age=age,date=date)],dose="All people (FOIA)")

buck=rbind(buck,vax,all)
buck=buck[,.(date=date,alive=ma(alive,10),dead=ma(dead,10)),by=.(age=age,dose=dose)]

xy=aggregate(list(pop=buck$alive,y=buck$dead/buck$alive*365e5*std[buck$age]/sum(std)),list(x=buck$date,z=buck$dose),sum,na.rm=T)

cz=aggregate(list(y=d$dead/d$pop*365e5*std[factor(d$age)]/sum(std)),data.frame(x=d$date),sum)
xy=rbind(xy,cbind(cz,pop=NA,z="All people (Czech Statistical Office)"))

xy$z=factor(xy$z,unique(xy$z))

xy$y[xy$pop<2e3]=NA

xstart=as.Date("2020-1-1");xend=as.Date("2023-1-1")
cand=c(sapply(c(1,2,5),\(x)x*10^c(-10:10)))
ystep=cand[which.min(abs(cand-max(xy$y,na.rm=T)/5))]
ystart=0
yend=ystep*ceiling(max(xy$y,na.rm=T)/ystep)
yend2=100;ystep2=25;secmult=yend/yend2

color=c(hcl(c(210,120,60,0,300)+15,90,50),"black","gray45","gray70")
fill=c(hcl(c(210,120,60,0,300)+15,80,70),"black","gray45","gray70")

label=data.frame(x=xstart+.02*(xend-xstart),y=seq(yend,,-yend/15,nlevels(xy$z))-yend/16,label=levels(xy$z))
