An unholy trilogy of preprints by Bruce Rapley and Matt Shelton

In January 2026 Bruce Rapley and Matt Shelton uploaded a series of three preprint about the clots, titled "Morphological and Histological Characterisation of Anomalous Intravascular Casts (AICs)", "Elemental Characterisation of Anomalous Intravascular Casts Reveals an Abnormal Biochemical Matrix", and "Proteomic Characterisation of Anomalous Intravascular Casts Reveals Non-Canonical Fibrin Architecture and Impaired Fibrinolysis". [https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202601.1846, https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202601.2149, https://www.preprints.org/manuscript/202601.2319]

I'm aware of only one earlier scientific paper about the calamari clots, which was coauthored by Daniel Santiago and John Oller and published in Oller's journal IJVTPR. Rapley, Shelton, and Santiago were all credited as members of the "scientific research and advisory team" of An Unholy Triad.

The new series of three preprints was basically a rehashing of the "top-secret press release" by Solution of Scientists from September 2024. [https://laurakasner.substack.com/p/how-the-white-clots-are-formed] The authors of the press release were anonymous, even though Laura Kasner said that Greg Harrison was one of the authors, so I don't know if Rapley or Shelton were also involved in writing the press release.

Bruce Rapley's affiliation was listed as "Consulting Scientist - Human Health and Environment, Uranus Consulting, New Zealand", and his correspondence email was analyst@uranusconsulting.org. When I Googled for "uranus consulting" "rapley", the only hits were about the new preprints. Uranus Consulting has a website with no content. [https://uranusconsulting.org] It sounds like a proctological practice, or maybe it refers to the source the authors consulted to derive their wisdom from.

The methods section of the proteomics paper says: "Raw LC–MS/MS data were processed using standard proteomic analysis pipelines to identify peptides and assign them to corresponding human proteins under established false discovery rate thresholds." It's unusual how the authors said they used standard pipelines but didn't describe the specific utilities they used.

The proteomics paper said "HPLC–MS/MS analysis identified a total of 541 human proteins across four independently", in the same way that the old press release said that "541 proteins, all of human origin, were discovered".

However now the SOS guys seem to have finally realized that fibrin is not supposed to show up in HPLC results, because they no longer said that a lack of fibrin is somehow anomalous.

The proteomics paper said: "A complete list of all 541 identified human proteins, together with their relative abundances, is provided in Supplementary Table S1." However the supplementary information was not posted at Preprints.org.

A funding statement said: "This work received significant funding from the NZDSOS, and a number of private donors who wished to remain anonymous, to facilitate the cost of laboratory analysis."

The acknowledgements said: "Technical and analytical support: The authors thank the international laboratories that assisted with sample preparation, elemental analysis, and related laboratory procedures. These laboratories requested anonymity due to the nature of the work, and their request has been respected. Clinical, mortuary, or sample access support: The authors acknowledge the embalmers and undertakers from multiple countries who facilitated access to specimens, clinical context, and case material, and who cooperated during sample collection. Their wish to remain anonymous has been respected.
