Number of 6 aa matches between SARS-CoV-2 and HIV-1

Pradhan's matches to the first two so-called inserts are both 6 aa long. There are 9,648 unique 6 aa segments of the coding sequences of Wuhan-Hu-1. When I searched for all of them against the same set of 10,733 env protein sequences I looked at in the previous section, I got a perfect match for 204 segments, which is about 2% of all segments, even though I only looked at a fairly small set of env protein sequences and not all proteins of HIV:

$ curl -s 'https://eutils.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/entrez/eutils/efetch.fcgi?db=nucleotide&rettype=fasta_cds_aa&id=MN908947'>sars2.aa
$ seqkit sliding -W6 -s1 sars2.aa|seqkit rmdup -s|seqkit locate -Pf- HIV1_ALL_2022_env_PRO.ungap>temp
$ sed 1d temp|awk '!a[$3]++'|wc -l
204

Next I searched GenBank for protein sequences that matched the query 0:2019[dp] "Human immunodeficiency virus 1"[porgn:__txid11676]. [https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/protein/?term=0%3A2019%5Bdp%5D+%22Human+immunodeficiency+virus+1%22%5Bporgn%3A%5f%5ftxid11676%5D] There were a total of 1,147,397 results, which consisted of a set of 123,456 unique protein sequences, which is similar to the set of HIV protein sequences that were included in the nr BLAST database at the time Pradhan did his BLAST searches in January 2020. Downloading the resutls with efetch failed, so I downloaded the results from the web GUI by seleting "Send to:", selecting "File", and changing "Format" to "FASTA". There were now perfect matches for 1,234 out of 9,648 of the 6 aa segments:

So if a 6 aa match counts as an "HIV insert", then there are not only HIV inserts at the spots of the genome of SARS-CoV-2 that look like inserts relative to SARS-CoV, but there are HIV inserts throughout the genome.
