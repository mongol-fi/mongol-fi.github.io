Ethical Skeptic's 66-gigabyte model with over 550 modules

In March 2026 people on Twitter found estimates of global cancer mortality from the Global Burden of Disease dataset by IMHE, where many countries had a sharp turn upwards in cancer mortality after 2020 or 2021. [https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/death-rate-from-cancer-for-15-to-49-year-olds, https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/death-rate-from-cancer-gbd?tab=line&country=GBRFRANZLJPNESP#all-charts] The estimates were calculated using opaque methodology, and there's no code you can run to reproduce the estimates. [https://www.healthdata.org/research-analysis/library/global-regional-and-national-burden-cancer-1990-2023-forecasts-2050] The estimates look different from actual cancer ASMR on a national level, where generally cancer mortality has continued to decline in the 2020s (or at least that's the case in the United States, UK, Japan, Australia, and Sweden).

Ethical Skeptic declared that he had now been vindicated, because IMHE's estimates matched his plots where he gets massive inflections in cancer mortality by either falsifying data or by applying undocumented adjustments like "excess MCOD normalization": [https://x.com/EthicalSkeptic/status/2033994979157127639]

Then ES wrote: "Instead of arrogantly demanding the publisher of this chart post their 'code on GitHub' - I went and pulled the data and re-created the inflections myself." But he didn't reproduce the methodology for calculating IMHE's estimates, but he just plotted the precalculated data. So the methodology is still opaque.

Then ironically someone commented: "To be fair, we need reproducible science. For too long so-called scientists have been publishing articles without details to reproduce their experiments. They hide behind IP. That's how we got into this mess and all this fake climate change science. Every paper should produce software to regenerate their results, period IMHO". Maybe they didn't realize that ES himself refuses to document his methodology, so ES interpreted the comment as an attack against himself, and he said "My model is 66 Gigs, over 550 modules and 4,760 files", so he couldn't post it on GitHub, because even though it would be possible for him to upload all of his files to GitHub, "it is the synthesis of the overall model which will not import" to Github: [https://x.com/EthicalSkeptic/status/2034355879282196800]
